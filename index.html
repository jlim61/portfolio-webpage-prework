About Me

Hi and welcome to my page! My name is Joshua Lim and I'm 26 years old. I grew up in Lake Forest, California which is very close to Irvine. There was always something to do growing up as I grew up with three other siblings: two older sisters and one older brother. That's right, I am the baby of the family! I started school in Tustin, but my family moved to Lake Forest when I was in 2nd grade and ended up going through Saddleback Valley Unified School District, finally graduating from El Toro High School.

Coming out of high school, there didn't really seem to be a serious interest for me in terms of my career goals. I wanted to be a police officer, but that was always shut down for being too dangerous. I ultimately ended up being pushed to Business Finance by my parents because of the stability they saw in it. I went to the Univeristy of Illinois at Chicago (UIC). While studying Business Finance, I found myself hating it the more I tried to learn it. It was my 3rd year of college that my sister helped me get into physical therapy because she noticed I had an interest in fitness. Athletico was the company that gave me my first taste of physical therapy. I ended up graduating with my finance degree, but ended up attending MiraCosta Community College to complete my prerequisite courses for a physical therapy program. I moved to Arizona to attend Midwestern University in 2021 for a physical therapy program and, although I found success in all materials related to physical therapy, ther was a lot of material to learn simply because it was a doctorate program. I dropped out as I wasn't happy with the program and came back to California and pursued work in Human Resources. I enjoyed it at first, but day after day of just so much negative information led me to being stressed and more irritable outside of work.

I am currently engaged to Chrstine, my long time girlfriend from when I was in Chicago. The only pet I have is my dog, Rio, who is currently 2 about to be 3. He was found as a dumped dog in Amarillo, Texas by my sister and me when I was helping her move back home from Chicago. He was estimated to be 3 months old when we found him on December 23, 2020 with an estimated birthday of September 20, 2020. Christine is my sister's best friend and I found Rio while helping my sister. I think it's safe to say that my sister is a good luck charm for me to find meaningful people.

Why Coding?

My journey has led me through various industries. I went from finance, to physical therapy, and then to human resources. That would lead many to the question, "why coding?" My first experience for any kind of coding was RPG maker. I know it's not real coding myself, but I dove a bit into RPG maker and the hobby of fan made Pokémon games. I think of it as a very basic form of coding because it involved a lot of typing inputs to make the program change things or to make certain sprites cause specific interactions. I spent several hours just messing around and seeing what I could do, but that got put on a bit of a backburner once I started working. As mentioned earlier in this page, I started feeling unhappy in HR and it start affecting my personal life so I knew I would have to pivot to a different career path.

One of my friends was taking a career change and starting to learn coding which got me interested in coding again. I spoke with some friends who are currently working as software developers or have already initiated a career change themselves. They told me what they liked and what they didn't like, but it seemed like they had more pros than cons. I soon found myself diving into it again, finding the same passion I did the first time around and seeing my friends so much happier than I was made me start trying my hand at it again. I started to work a bit on free courses from Codecademy that on of my friends recommended I try. The realization finally hit me that I kept putting it off because I was scared of how others might perceive me. I think the idea of still trying to find my career path after what feels like a long time after graduating from college made me wonder what others might think of me. I thought people might think it embarassing that I'm still not where I think I should be in my life by now or maybe they would tell me that it's a bad idea and that I should avoid the risk if it doesn't work out. In reality, after mentioning it to friends and family, everyone was really supportive and that gave me more confidence to make the choice now.
